Lead Generation and its Importance in Your Business Growth

By Arjun Bingipur

Whatever is the size and nature of your business, lead generation is one of the important tools that is commonly used by management and marketing teams. In the following post, we hope to answer some of those questions, including what lead generation is and how it can help to grow your business.

What is Lead Generation?

Lead generation is the process involved in creating interest for potential customers by supplying them with relevant and enticing information about your services and/or products. The aim is simple — develop enough interest so that they want to contact you or even buy your products or book your services.

Lead generation tools are classified into the following categories:

  • Lead data collection & email lookup
  • Email follow-up & marketing
  • Landing page creation & capture
  • Complete marketing automation
  • Live chat & social media
  • Customer research & calendar scheduling

By making use of these tools, you not only capture but also convert more leads.

Generates Traffic

One of the most crucial ways that lead generation helps your business growth is by generating inbound enquiries or traffic if you have a web presence. These inbound enquiries or website visitors are your potential customers or prospects. When your lead generation efforts are effective, these prospects will convert to become your customers.

More Focused Targeting of Customers

When executed correctly, lead generation can target the customers that are actively looking for products or services like yours. You can use a lead generation funnel to systematically generate leads — funnelling your target audience through different stages until they decide to make a purchase.

Helps Your Sales and Marketing Teams

Two teams in your business that need to work cohesively are marketing and sales. The incentive of having an efficient lead generation campaign is often the reason for your sales and marketing teams to be aligned. Sales teams will have the opportunity to engage with a healthy pipeline because of a well-executed lead generation campaign from the marketing team.

Helps to Generate Greater Brand Awareness

Lead generation can help increase your brand awareness as it educates your target audience about your products or services and their features, creating that all-important "word of mouth". Lead nurturing — building relationships with prospects at every stage of the funnel — builds credibility and trust from being an expert in your field.

People & Culture

Benefits of Employee Engagement Programs

By Arjun Bingipur

Have you thought about what your company's biggest asset is? More than the services or products you offer, it's your employees. Without them, you wouldn't be able to offer the services and products you do. So you must establish and maintain a working environment where they can thrive. Increasing employee engagement is something that can really make or break your business, but it is an aspect of running a business that is not understood properly and is often overlooked.

What Does Employee Engagement Mean?

It is basically a workplace approach that provides the best conditions for all employees to give their best every day. It motivates them to offer full commitment towards meeting the values and goals of the company and contributing to the organisation's overall success while giving their self-confidence and self-worth a boost. Employee engagement programs are courses designed to make increasing employee engagement easier.

Helps Improve Employee Retention

Not only will an appropriate employee engagement program help your company retain its employees, but it will also help you to keep the employees that perform the best. Employees that are fully engaged tend to have greater loyalty towards their employers because they get greater job satisfaction from their position.

Establishes a More Harmonious Working Environment

When there are too many employees who feel disengaged from the business, it can have a terrible effect on your working environment. When you invest in employee engagement, you increase team morale and make all members feel valued.

Improves Teamwork and Productivity

When you put the time and effort into increasing employee engagement in your company, it can have a staggering effect on how well everyone works together. Employee engagement programs are designed to help improve teamwork and productivity. When people are happy, they tend to work and perform better — and people feel happier when they are valued.

Attract a Better Quality of Future Employees

Employee engagement that leads to a happier and more stable workforce is not something that will just be appreciated internally. Candidates will be more attracted to a business that treats its workforce well. Not only will you find it easier attracting fresh new talent, but you will also attract a better quality of employee — enthusiastic, passionate and qualified.

Employee Engagement Results in Business Success

In summary, your business will succeed when there's a strong and effective team. When you know how to pick, manage and lead a team of talented people, your business can reach the goals you've set. It is therefore critical to develop a great team in order to grow your business.

What is Market Positioning and How Important is it in Your Business?

By Arjun Bingipur

The key to standing out in today's competitive business climate is to embrace branding. For businesses that prioritise branding, profits can improve by as much as 23%, but it is not just about the revenue. Improved branding means a better market position while improving customer loyalty and reliability. Branding and market positioning are symbiotic — for those companies that utilise a robust branding strategy combined with a strong focus on improving market positioning, growth is far easier to achieve.

What is Market Positioning?

At its most basic, market positioning is when you firmly establish the identity of your company or product so that it has more immediate recognition and a specific perception. A clean example would be Mercedes — immediately recognisable as a luxury car. Having the right market positioning strategies means knowing who your target audience is so that you can target your branding more accurately. Even small businesses that establish their positioning statement can compete with the largest companies in the world.

Identifying Your Target Market

All market positioning strategies will first require you to learn more about your target audience. By focusing on a specific target audience, you can aim your marketing at the people who are likely to buy from you or hire your services. To define your target market:

  • Existing Customers — identify commonalities and learn what drives them to spend
  • Competitor Research — look for niches not being served by current brands
  • Know Your Product — list the best features and brainstorm the audiences who value them
  • Identifying Demographics — fine-tune by age, geography, income, gender, education and more

Determining Your Current Market Position

Before you start fine-tuning your branding and marketing, you need to identify exactly what your current market position is. Your unique selling point (USP) is what sets you apart from your competitors and is the key to your effective branding. You will need to identify those USPs that attract customers because they want or need what you offer — aim to be the only business that offers that exact solution.

The Importance of Niche Audiences

When consumers do not understand your brand, it can take a lot of time, energy, and marketing budget to change their minds. The goal of your positioning strategy should be to align the perception of your brand with what you want it to be. Differentiating yourself from your competitors is crucial — find those niches and customers that are not getting what they need from your competitors and fill those gaps.

Positioning Your Business

Once you have identified your USP and finely tuned your branding, it is time to use those to work on improving your market position. Your marketing will be more effective because it will be more likely to resonate with your target audience. Use your customer data to find out where and when your customers spend their time, and focus your efforts on those channels for maximum return.

How To Make The Most Of Strategic Planning?

By Arjun Bingipur

There is a famous quote, “A goal without a plan is just a wish.” A goal can only really be achieved when hard work, time, resources and structure are put into place. One element that can help a company achieve its growth goals is strategic planning. So, what is strategic planning, and how can it help you?

Understanding the Basics of Strategic Planning

Strategic planning is a systematic process of envisioning a future goal and defining a strategy that works for an individual company or organisation. It involves making decisions on important aspects such as allocating expenses and dividing a future vision into manageable goals, often as a step-by-step sequence for moving from one point to another.

How Is Strategic Planning Different From Long-Term Planning?

Strategic planning is not the same as long-term planning. Long-term planning sets out how the strategic plan is achieved and is usually given a timeframe, often around five years. The exact period depends on the company’s vision and objectives. Both are important, but they serve different purposes.

How Can Strategic Planning Help Your Business?

When you are interested in growing your business, defining your goals is essential. Strategic planning helps you establish:

  • What your company is missing, such as stronger team productivity
  • What you want, such as more leads and increased revenue
  • What you need to achieve, such as better customer engagement

Without defining these areas, you could work hard without seeing the right results. Strategic planning also provides clear communication across the company, defines the overall business mission and aligns goals throughout the organisation.

Forming Your Strategic Plan

Start by understanding the current situation of your business. Review staff productivity, customer engagement, expenses and whether your mission statement still reflects the organisation. A detailed audit of your strengths, weaknesses, opportunities and threats is an essential starting point.

Next, decide where you want to take the business. Turn the areas needing improvement into goals, connect related factors and define the strategies needed to achieve each objective. Review the plan, make any necessary changes, then put it into action and monitor progress.

Build a Plan That Works for You

Your strategy needs to fit your business. Strategic planning templates and examples can provide a useful starting point, while professional guidance can help you shape a plan around your specific circumstances and goals.

Strategic Planning Beyond Growth

Strategic planning works for many objectives, not only growth. Your goal might be to open locations in new countries, improve sustainability or transform how the company operates. Once you understand the process, you can apply it to a wide range of business ambitions.
